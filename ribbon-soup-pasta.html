Ribbon and Soup Pasta

Long, hearty noodles.

Anelli is a thin, ring shaped pasta! It's perfect for soup, salad, or baked dishes to add a bit of heartiness to a meal. Fun fact, "Anelli" actually means "small rings" in Italian.

Also known as "Gigli", this ruffled cone-shaped pasta is the perfect vessel for sauces. This pasta shape originates from Tuscany, where "Gigli" can be translated to "Lillies"

Cascatelli is a new pasta shaped released in 2021! Invented by Dan Pashman, cascatelli is touted as an ideal shape for holding suace without falling off your fork. In Italian, "Cascatelli" means "waterfall"!

Fideo is Spanish for pasta. This thin, short noodle is used in many Spanish and Mexican recipes. Pair it with fresh veggies for a pop of color!

A more well-known pasta shape, lasagne is a flat, sheet like pasta that is often layered between meat, cheese, and sauce.

Linguine is a long, slightly wider noodle that pairs well with seafood dishes. A personal favorite dish of mine is linguine with clams and white sauce!

Orzo is a short and flat pasta with a similar grain-like style to rice. Orzo is primarily used in soup or salad based dishes, and in Italian can be translated as "barley" due it's visual similarity with grains of barley.

Tagliatelle is a long, wide noodle similar to fettuccine. It is often used in hearty pasta-based dishes, as the width of the noodle compliments well with chunkier pasta sauces.

In Italian, spaghetti can be translated as "a length of cord". It's long, thin shape pairs perfectly with classic dishes like spaghetti and meatballs!
